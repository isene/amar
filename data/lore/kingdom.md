# The Kingdom of Amar

Source: [d6gaming.org/The_Kingdom_of_Amar](https://d6gaming.org/index.php/The_Kingdom_of_Amar)

The Kingdom of Amar, founded **354 years ago**, is "a fairly peaceful kingdom" with "a stable society and its problems are mostly predictable." The realm is geographically isolated by fog to the east and south, making escape practically impossible.

- **Total population**: ~50,000 humans
- **Noble population**: ~900
- **Capital**: Amaron (~18,000)

## Government

- **King Caolain II** — state affairs, tax collection. Walmaer worshipper; big and sturdy, somewhat rude but trying to be elegant.
- **Queen Fiona** — spiritual magistrate. Shalissa priestess; very elegant and spiritually aware.
- **Prince Vaiangor** (age 333) — sports enthusiast; Recolar devotee; idol figure for healthy living.
- **Princess Ianira** (age 331) — artist (singer, painter, poet, flautist); Ielina initiate.
- **Commander Seillan Torthal** — military leader and Taroc warlord.

The King directly oversees Amaronir; five Barons govern the other districts.

## Magick in the Kingdom

Only **1 in 50 humans** possesses Magical Aptitude (MA). Among those:

- 1 in 3 develop a **Womp** (magical focus)
- 1 in 2 of those learn spells

Between **300 and 400 citizens** practice magick across various paths.

**The Magick Circle** consists of eight powerful wizards led by **The Rai**, a summoning specialist.

**Black magick is banned** by royal decree.

## Religions

Primary faiths in the kingdom: **Walmaer, Shalissa, Anashina, Alesia, Taroc, Juba**.

Nobles **exclusively** worship **MacGillan and Gwendyll**.

**Cursed and avoided**: Man Peggon, Elesi, Mestronorpha, Maleko.

## Underground Organizations

### The Calah
Rebel movement opposing the King; often Tzankili (Tsankili) worshippers.

### Dark Dagger
Assassination guild; rumoured Kraagh / Mestronorpha worshippers.

### The Cloaks
Thieves' guild with mandatory membership; mostly Tzankili devotees.

## Notable Locations

### Within the Kingdom

- **The Eliminator** — underground maze beneath the royal castle. Annual trial; one survivor in ten gains nobility.
- **The Rift** — premium adventurer inn run by warrior **Sharina** (Eliminator survivor, Recolar worshipper). Excellent service for regulars.
- **The Magick Cauldron** — inn frequented by would-be magicians.
- **The Challenge Ground** — annual wizard dueling arena (Spell Magick Day).
- **The Round Hill** — meditation site providing peace and relaxation.
- **The Oracle of Varni** — sacred pond with a wise tree spirit; answers yes/no questions.
- **Academica Magicka** (Amaron) — wizard academy.
- **The Coliseum** (Amaron) — gladiatorial games.

### Beyond the Kingdom

- **The Magick Ring** — ring of oak trees in faerie woods; offers life-changing interventions.
- **The Dead Marsh** — cursed swamp where the necromancer Arachan fought the King; the dead rise as zombies.
- **The Dark Woods** — evil forest between troll and faerie realms; corrupted faeries, witches, prevalent black magic.
- **The Water Maze** — island labyrinth home to the water dragon **Aia**.
- **The Circle's Tower** — invisible, invulnerable wizard tower; inspires fear.
- **The Old Dwarven Caves** — demon-infested mines; source of lost dwarven treasures.

## Notable Personalities

### Merchants

- **Zarin** — travelling alchemist; merchant of useful potions with fair pricing.
- **Naraghin** — wizard with a magick shop; trades spells; has poor memory.

### Adventuring Parties

- **The Wayanah Party** — high-level: Wayanah (warrior-sorceress), Hina (warrior), Mizamir (archeress), Boran (fighter), Lagioracharan (scout).
- **Three Playful Wizards** — Povi (Air), Ferro (Fire), Manir (Life). Practice pranks on each other from towers 100 m apart.
- **The Guards** (mercenary) — Kator (knight, leader), Eris (priestess), Vinar (ranger), Orach (warrior), Yaloosi (wizard).
- **Ran-Asar & Ayah** — respected adventurers with the puma **Mira**; pursue mysterious missions.

## Geography & Economy

The kingdom is bordered by mountains to the north (dwarf territory), forests and the Araxi to the west, and fog off the southern and eastern shores. Climate is **comparable to France**; planetary axis tilt is 15° (less seasonal variation than Earth).

**External trade**: humans export food, wine, clothing to dwarves; dwarves provide metals, weapons, armor, jewelry.

**Internal goods**: fish, amber, fur, horses, spices, pearls.
